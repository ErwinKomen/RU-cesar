The Cesar web application provides a low-level way to do syntactic research in annotated text corpora.

Latest news

The Cesar back-end will be down in the Summer: 6/Jul/2018 - 30/Jul/2018.

Cesar itself will be running, but there are a number of things you won't be able to do:

  • Browsing: many texts will not be browsable. You will be able to see some, if they have been pre-loaded into the Cesar interface.
  • Syntax: viewing the syntax of sentences will not be possible.
  • Running: searches will not run, because the back-end is down.
  • Results: your stored results may be visible to some extent. Most of the results won't be visible, however.

Getting started

The CESAR application has been designed by the Technical Service Group of the Humanities Lab at the Radboud University Nijmegen. Any researcher is invited to register and maintain his or her own research projects. Projects of one researcher are by default not visible to other researchers, but it is possible to specify a different accessibility level.
